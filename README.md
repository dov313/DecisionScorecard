# The Intelligent Decision Scorecard

There are a lot of decisions to be made in business and in life.

Should we start this project? Should we hire this person? Should we invest in this opportunity? Should we change direction? Should we say yes to this idea, test it small, save it for later, or simply say no?

The hard part is not always having ideas. Most people have plenty of ideas. The hard part is knowing which ideas deserve time, money, focus, and energy.

So the question is:

**How can we make better decisions and feel more confident that we are choosing the right move?**

That is why we created **The Intelligent Decision Scorecard**.

This scorecard is inspired by decision-making principles connected to some of the greatest business thinkers and operators, including **Jeff Bezos, Elon Musk, Warren Buffett, Steve Jobs, and Charlie Munger**.

But this is not a complicated framework that takes hours to complete. It is a simple scoring system that helps you think through a decision in a clear and practical way.

Answer a few questions, score your decision, and see what the result says.

---

## What Is The Intelligent Decision Scorecard?

The Intelligent Decision Scorecard is a simple website that helps you evaluate a decision before you commit time, money, focus, or energy to it.

Instead of relying only on emotion, pressure, excitement, or overthinking, the scorecard gives you a structured way to look at the decision from different angles.

At the end, you receive a score that helps you understand whether the decision looks strong, needs a smaller test, should be saved for later, or may not be worth doing right now.

---

## Why We Built It

Most people do not struggle because they have no options. They struggle because they have too many options and no simple way to judge them.

Without a clear decision framework, it is easy to chase too many ideas, start projects that never get finished, waste money, overcommit, or say yes to things that quietly pull you away from what matters most.

The Intelligent Decision Scorecard was built to make that process easier. It helps you slow down for a few minutes, think clearly, and make a better call.

---

## How It Works

The process is simple:

1. Enter the decision you are considering.
2. Answer a short set of guided questions.
3. Score the decision based on the prompts.
4. Get a final result.
5. Use the result to decide your next move.

The scorecard does not make the decision for you. It gives you a clearer way to think about the decision before you act.

---

## What The Score Helps You Decide

The final score helps place the decision into one of four possible outcomes:

| Result | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Do it now | The decision looks strong and worth acting on. |
| Test it small | The decision has potential, but should be tested before going all in. |
| Save it for later | The decision may be useful, but probably not right now. |
| Say no | The decision is likely a distraction or not worth the current cost. |

---

## When To Use It

Use The Intelligent Decision Scorecard when you are considering something that has a real cost. That cost could be money, time, attention, energy, focus, team capacity, or opportunity cost.

It can be used for business decisions, project decisions, hiring decisions, investment decisions, strategic decisions, personal commitments, or any situation where you want more clarity before saying yes.

---

## Who It Is For

This tool is for founders, business owners, operators, creators, professionals, and anyone who wants to make better decisions without overcomplicating the process.

It is especially useful when a decision feels exciting, uncertain, expensive, risky, or distracting.

---

## Why This Matters

Not every idea deserves action.

Some ideas should be done now. Some should be tested small. Some should be saved for later. Some should be ignored.

The Intelligent Decision Scorecard helps you see the difference.

It is not here to replace your judgment. It is here to improve it.

---

## Final Thought

Better decisions do not always come from thinking longer.

Sometimes they come from thinking more clearly.

The Intelligent Decision Scorecard gives you a simple structure to do exactly that.
